The paradox

Here's the thing about the archive: almost all of it disappears.

Only 2.1% of data generated is retained past its first year. The rest—ephemeral messages, temporary files, streaming buffers—vanishes like it never existed. We're building the largest library in history, and most of it burns the moment it's written.

The public archive isn't a permanent record. It's a river—constantly flowing, constantly forgetting.
